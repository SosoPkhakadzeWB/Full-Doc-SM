⚡ Setter Engine

Manages everything from the moment a lead opts in to the moment they're booked on a call. Controls Speed to Lead, delegation, call tracking, and Smart View priority queues.

🙋 For Non-Technical Team: The Setter Engine is your "traffic control." It decides which leads go to which setter, tracks how fast setters call new leads, and organizes leads into priority lists (P1 → P4). When you dial and log calls in Aloware, this engine counts them automatically.

How the Setter Engine Works — Step by Step

1
Lead opts in — submits a form (VSL or Quiz funnel). Entry Point Mapping fires immediately.
2
Lead is categorized — added to the right funnel list (List 2173 or 2174), Lead Status set to NEW, lifecycle set to Lead.
3
Delegation stamp — Delegation workflow fires to record the exact business-hours timestamp when the lead was assigned. This starts the Speed to Lead clock.
4
Setter dials in Aloware — Universal Call Tracker increments the call count and stamps the last dial time automatically.
5
First call is stamped — First Outbound Call Date is captured. Speed to Lead = First Call Date − Delegation Date.
6
STL bucket assigned — lead is bucketed: <30 min, <2 hrs, <24 hrs, 24–48 hrs, or 72+ hrs for dashboard reporting.
7
Lead Refresh — after dialing, setters trigger Lead Refresh to hide the lead for a cooldown period before it reappears in Smart Views.
8
Application qual — if lead submits application, investment amount routes them: <$1k = UNQUALIFIED, $1k+ = MQL.
9
LT purchase — if lead buys a low-ticket offer, they enter PQL status (highest priority pre-SQL). PQL list triggers priority dialing.

P1
New Hot Leads
Fresh opt-ins from today. Lead Status = NEW. Sorted by Lead Score descending. These are the highest priority — call within 30 minutes.
Filters: Setter Owner = Me · Lead Status = NEW · Created Today · Lead Refresh Active ≠ Yes
P2
Follow-Ups
Leads where a meaningful conversation has started but no call is booked yet. Keep in touch until they commit to a call or go cold.
Filters: Setter Owner = Me · Lead Status = IN_PROGRESS
P3
Active Re-Engagement
Older leads showing recent activity signals (email opens, site visits). Lead Score ≥ threshold. Worth calling on busy dial days.
Filters: Lead Score ≥ threshold · Recent Activity Signal
P4
Cold Leads
Older leads with PROSPECTING or BAD_TIMING status. Lowest priority but still worth dialing on slow days or after P1–P3 are cleared.
Filters: Lead Status = PROSPECTING or BAD_TIMING · Lead Refresh Active ≠ Yes

📅 Show Rate Engine

Ensures booked meetings actually happen. Manages confirmation tracking, pre-call communication, booking advance analysis, and appointment outcome dispositions.

🙋 For Non-Technical Team: The Show Rate Engine is your "appointment guardian." Once a call is booked, this engine tracks whether leads confirm, sends reminders, and records what happened (showed, no-showed, canceled). Your daily confirmation view comes from this engine.

How the Show Rate Engine Works

1
Appointment created — Big Brain creates the Appointment record when a booking is made. It lands in "Upcoming Appointments" stage automatically.
2
Confirmation List stamped — the Confirmation List workflow checks which time-based list the contact is in (48+ hrs, 24–48 hrs, <24 hrs, confirmed, never confirmed) and stamps the appointment property accordingly.
3
Confirmation Disposition fires — based on the stamped list, the pipeline stage moves automatically. Confirmed? Stage → Confirmed. Never Confirmed? Stage → Never Confirmed. Canceled? Routed to cancellation workflow.
4
Pre-call comms — email/SMS sequences fire based on confirmation status and time-to-appointment (currently disabled, ready to activate).
5
5-minute "In Progress" flag — the Appointment — In Progress? workflow fires 5 minutes before start time, flagging the call as active.
6
Booking Advance Range stamped — on creation, the time between booking date and appointment date is bucketed (Under 24h, 24–48h, etc.) for show rate correlation reporting.
7
Rep dispositions the call — rep sets "Final Outcome of Appointment" to Completed / No Show / Canceled. Final Disposition workflow fires and archives the record.
8
Association labels update — "Disco In Progress" or "Closer In Progress" label swaps to "Past Disco" or "Past Closer," removing the appointment from active views.

🎯 Closer Engine

Drives all deal stage automation in the Sales Momentum Pipeline. From Calendly integration and meeting deduplication to Red Zone alerts, Closed Won onboarding, and finance approvals.

🙋 For Non-Technical Team: The Closer Engine is your "deal brain." Every time you move a deal between stages in HubSpot, this engine automatically updates contact records, sends Slack alerts, stamps dates, and routes deals. Filling out the Conditional Stage Properties (orange box fields) before moving is required — the system depends on that data.

Closer Engine — How It Works

1
Calendly booking detected — Zapier writes URI to Contact. Calendly Integration workflow fires to clean duplicates, fetch meeting details, parse meeting type, and set booking method.
2
Big Brain fires — creates Appointment record, moves deal to Closing Call Booked, assigns Closer owner, sends Slack confirmation alert.
3
Deal stage changes — Sales Big Brain Stage Automation fires on every stage change. 13 branches handle all outcomes: outcomes written, lifecycle updated, lead status set, dates stamped.
4
Red Zone reached — 🚩 Slack alert fires. Deal Type + Red Zone Type + Expected Close Date all required before moving. Appears in forecasting views.
5
Pending Approval — 5 required fields must be filled (Amount, Financing, Lead Type, Payment Plan). Zapier webhook fires to #Sales-Approval Slack channel.
6
Closed Won — Both Closing Conditions (Agreement Signed + Payment Made) must be checked. Marketing subscription opt-in fires, lifecycle → customer, closedate stamped.
⚠️ Closer Daily Workflow Priority: 1. Work active appointments (In Progress or confirmed for today) 2. Red Zone deals — sorted by Expected Close Date 3. Closer Nurture deals — sorted by Next Touch Date 4. Closing Qualified deals 5. No Show / Canceled follow-ups
